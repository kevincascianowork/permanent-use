Custodial land design for those who want more from life.

Most land mistakes aren’t aesthetic.
They’re functionally flawed, and useless.

Permanent Use exists to see second- and third-order consequences before anything is built—so the land remains coherent, durable, and usable long after initial intentions fade.

This is design-only work.

Services

Work is sold as finite engagements. Each one has a defined scope, deliverables, and end date.

1) Site Assessment (Required Entry)

What it is: A paid on-site judgment call before any design work.

  • Scope: One on-site visit (half day maximum)
  • Soil / water / access / constraints scan
  • Failure points, leverage points, and non-negotiables
  • A clear decision: proceed, pause, or leave it alone
  • Output: Written assessment memo + a recommended next step
  • Fee: $5,000–$10,000

Required for new clients.

2) Master Plan & Phasing (Core)

What it is: The governing document for the property.

  • Scope: Land-use plan + build sequence (Year 1 / Year 3 / Year 5)
  • Edible landscape systems + soil strategy
  • Water systems, compost, micro-production zones (as appropriate)
  • Budget + ROI logic: directional ranges only (no contractor bidding)
  • Maintenance reality included. No fantasy timelines.
  • Deliverables: Annotated plans, diagrams, written strategy, decision-ready documentation
  • Fee: $25,000–$75,000+ (higher for complex sites, estates, or institutions)

Rule: Design only. No building or labor management.

3) Build Review (Compliance Checks)

What it is: Scheduled checkpoints against the plan.

  • Scope: Sequencing review + contractor selection support (as needed)
  • Pre-defined site visits (fixed count)
  • Redlines, corrections, and punch-list notes against the master plan
  • Structure: Fixed package (not a retainer)
  • Fee: $7,500–$30,000+ (based on complexity and visit count)

Rule: I am not supervising subcontractors. I verify alignment with the plan.

4) Stewardship Review (Selective)

What it is: For clients who want durable performance over time, without turning the relationship into a subscription.

  • Scope: Seasonal review session(s)
  • Soil indicators + system performance check
  • Risk prevention updates + optimization recommendations
  • Structure: Sold per review (no standing retainer)
  • Fee: $5,000–$15,000 per review cycle
